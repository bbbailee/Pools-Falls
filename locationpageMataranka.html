Mataranka Springs
Northern Territory
4hr from Darwin

Mataranka Falls is a picturesque natural attraction located in the Northern Territory of Australia. Situated within Elsey National Park, this serene oasis is renowned for its stunning landscapes, crystal-clear thermal pools, and lush surroundings. The falls are formed by the steady flow of water from the Roper River, which maintains a consistent temperature due to the geothermal activity in the area.

Visitors to Mataranka Falls are captivated by the inviting, turquoise-colored thermal pools that remain at a comfortable temperature year-round. The warm waters create a relaxing and soothing environment, making the falls a popular destination for both tourists and locals seeking to unwind and rejuvenate.

The park's verdant foliage, characterized by dense palm forests and tropical vegetation, adds to the allure of the site. The unique ecosystem supports a variety of wildlife, including fish, turtles, and an array of bird species, enhancing the overall natural experience.

"The most picturesque swim in the state"
Tip: You need a park pass to enter the area

Getting There
To get to Mataranka Falls in the Northern Territory, Australia from Darwin, you can follow these general directions;

Driving
 The most common way to reach Mataranka Falls is by driving. The distance between Darwin and Mataranka Falls is approximately 420 km, and the journey takes around 4.5 to 5.5 hours. Head south from Darwin on the Stuart Highway. After Katherine, Turn onto Roper Highway (Route 4) and follow the signs to Mataranka. Once you reach Mataranka, you'll find signs directing you to Elsey National Park and Mataranka Thermal Pool, where the Mataranka Falls are located.

Public Transport
 If you don't have access to a private vehicle, you can also consider taking a long-distance bus from Darwin to Katherine. From Katherine, you might need to arrange for local transportation to reach Mataranka Falls.

Tours and Guided Trips
 There are also tour operators that offer guided tours to Mataranka Falls from Darwin. These tours often include transportation, guided commentary, and sometimes additional stops at other attractions along the way.

Camping
Camping near Mataranka Falls offers a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the region and enjoy a tranquil outdoor experience. The falls are located within Elsey National Park, which provides camping facilities for visitors who want to stay close to the thermal pools and lush landscapes. Here's what you can expect when camping at Mataranka Falls:

Camping Sites
 Mataranka Homestead Campground This campground is located near Mataranka Homestead, offering convenient access to the thermal pools and other attractions. Facilities often include toilets, showers, barbeque areas, and picnic tables. The campground offers both powered and non-powered sites, suitable for tents, campervans, and caravans.

24-Hour Campground
 This campground is a more rustic option, situated near Bitter Springs, another thermal pool area. Facilities are more basic, with pit toilets and limited amenities. The campground is suitable for self-sufficient campers and those looking for a more remote camping experience.
