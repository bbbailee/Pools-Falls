Bondi Icebergs
New South Wales
20mins from Sydney CBD

Bondi Icebergs, situated in the iconic Bondi Beach in Sydney, is a renowned Australian landmark. It's not only a swimming club but also a cultural institution that has been part of Sydney's coastal identity for generations. The Icebergs offer a unique ocean pool experience, breathtaking ocean views, and a sense of community for its members and visitors.

Visiting Bondi Icebergs provides the opportunity to swim in a seawater pool right on the ocean's edge, with the backdrop of the rolling waves of Bondi Beach. It's a popular spot for swimmers, sunbathers, and those seeking a quintessential Australian beach experience.

"Iconic ocean pool at Bondi Beach with stunning coastal views."
Tip: There's an admission fee of $9 for adults and $6 for children

Getting There
Bondi Icebergs is conveniently accessible from the heart of Sydney, with options for both driving and public transport.

Driving
 If you prefer to drive, Bondi Beach is easily accessible from the city center, with ample parking available in the vicinity of the beach and Icebergs.

Public Transport
 Sydney's efficient public transport system, including buses and the Bondi Junction train station, provides easy access to Bondi Beach and the Icebergs.

Tours and Guided Trips
 While there may not be specific tours for Bondi Icebergs, you can explore the area independently or join broader Bondi Beach tours, which often include a stop at this iconic site.

Camping
Camping is not available at Bondi Icebergs, as it primarily functions as a day-use swimming club and restaurant.
