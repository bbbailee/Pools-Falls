Babinda Boulders
Queensland
1hr from Cairns

Babinda Boulders, nestled in the lush tropical rainforest of Far North Queensland, is a natural wonder renowned for its serene beauty and crystal-clear waters.

This idyllic destination is located in the vicinity of the small town of Babinda, approximately 60 kilometres south of Cairns. The star attraction here is a series of striking granite boulders that punctuate the babbling waters of Babinda Creek, creating inviting pools and cascading waterfalls.

Known as "The Devil's Pool," Babinda Boulders is not only a visual delight but also steeped in Aboriginal legend and is a favourite spot for nature enthusiasts and travellers seeking a tranquil retreat.

"Rainforest paradise for nature enthusiasts near Cairns."
Tip: Be careful of fast flowing currents

Getting There
Babinda Boulders is easily accessible from Cairns and the surrounding regions. There are two primary modes of transportation to consider: driving and public transport.

Driving
 If you prefer driving, you can take the scenic route south from Cairns on the Bruce Highway. The drive takes approximately an hour and offers stunning views of the coastal landscapes. Babinda Boulders is well-signposted, and there is ample parking available for visitors.

Public Transport
 For those without a car, you can take advantage of the reliable public transport options in the area. Several bus services connect Cairns to Babinda, making it a convenient choice for travelers using public transportation.

Tours and Guided Trips
 Many tour operators offer guided tours to Babinda Boulders, providing valuable insights into the natural and cultural aspects of the area. These tours can enhance your experience and ensure you make the most of your visit. They often include transportation and knowledgeable guides who share the rich history and Aboriginal legends associated with the boulders.

Camping
For those who wish to extend their stay and truly immerse themselves in the natural beauty of Babinda Boulders, camping is a fantastic option. Camping Sites
 Babinda Boulders features a well-maintained camping area with designated sites for tents and caravans. Facilities typically include toilets, picnic tables, and barbeque facilities. It's essential to check availability and make bookings in advance, as this serene camping spot can get busy, particularly during the holiday seasons.
